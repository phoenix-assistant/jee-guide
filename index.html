DTU — Delhi Technological University

GOVT JEE MAIN via JAC Delhi Est. 1941 · Rohini, Delhi

🔗 JAC Delhi Registration · DTU Official · OR-CR Reports

Eligibility: Pass Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry & Maths. No minimum % required by JAC Delhi (the 75% rule applies only to JoSAA/NITs, not DTU/NSUT).

Quota: 85% Delhi / 15% OSD. At 38K, only Delhi quota core/niche branches are possible.

Women Supernumerary: DTU has supernumerary seats for women (~20% increase over intake). Cutoffs are typically 1.5–2× the general closing rank — significantly easier to get in. Check GNSGD (Gender-Neutral Supernumerary Girls Delhi) column in OR-CR PDFs.

🔀 Other Admission Options at JIIT

  • Board Merit — JIIT also admits on Class 12 marks (without JEE). Apply on JIIT portal separately.

JIIT doesn't have competitive cutoffs like govt colleges. At 38K, you're in the top third of their admits — strong position for scholarship negotiation and better campus (Sector 62).

DASA — Direct Admission of Students Abroad

What is it? A separate admission track for NRI / PIO / OCI / foreign nationals into NITs, IIITs, and state-funded technical universities like DTU & NSUT. Supernumerary seats (over and above regular intake). Higher fees in USD. Significantly relaxed cutoffs.

⚠️ Common Misconception: "Anyone can apply to DASA if they pay in dollars"

NO. This is false. DASA has strict citizenship/residential eligibility. You cannot simply pay USD fees and get in. An ordinary Indian student living in India with Indian parents working in India cannot apply — regardless of willingness to pay higher fees.

Who is Eligible? (DASA 2026 Criteria)

Sources: Cross-referenced across Allen Overseas, TestprepKart, Sarvgyan, PW Gulf, and official DASA 2025 brochure. Official DASA 2026 brochure not yet released — verify at dasanit.org when available (~June 2026).

CategoryWho QualifiesResidential Requirement
Foreign National (FN)Citizen of any country except India (valid foreign passport)None. Can apply from anywhere, including India.
OCI Card HolderOverseas Citizen of India card holderNone. Can apply from anywhere.
PIO Card HolderPerson of Indian Origin card holderNone. Can apply from anywhere.
NRI (Indian Citizen abroad)Indian citizen with NRI status per FEMA (residing abroad for employment/business/education)Must have studied abroad for ≥2 years in the last 8 years, including Class XI & XII passed from abroad.
CIWG (Children of Indian Workers in Gulf)Child of an Indian worker employed in Gulf countries (UAE, Oman, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia)Parent must be working in eligible Gulf country. Student may have studied in India. Concessional fee: ₹1,25,000/semester (vs $4,000/semester for DASA).

⚠️ Key Changes Across Years (Official PDF Sources):

  • SAT no longer accepted. JEE Main is the sole entrance exam since DASA 2024. No SAT route.
  • Minimum marks: 75% aggregate (or 7.5 CGPA out of 10) in Class XII — confirmed in both 2024 and 2025 official brochures.
  • NRI residential requirement is strict: Must have studied Class XI & XII abroad (≥2 years in last 8 years). India-based students with NRI parents who work abroad but where the student studied in India — do NOT qualify unless the student actually attended school abroad for 2+ years.
  • CIWG still exists — confirmed in both 2024 and 2025 brochures. Children of Indian workers in Gulf countries get concessional fees (₹1,25,000/sem vs $4,000/sem). The student can have studied in India.
  • CIWG expanded in 2025: Parent can be currently working OR have worked till any date in 2025. In 2024, parent had to be currently working.
  • Age limit: Born on or after Oct 1, 1999 (2024) → Oct 1, 2000 (2025) → likely Oct 1, 2001 (2026).
  • Major 2025 structural change: DASA merged with CSAB-Special. Coordinated by NIT Rourkela (was NIT Raipur in 2024). Single portal for both. Runs AFTER JoSAA (late July), not before.

Who CANNOT Apply to DASA 2026

  • ❌ Indian citizen living in India, both parents in India, studied Class XI-XII in India — regardless of willingness to pay
  • ❌ Indian citizen with NRI uncle/aunt but student never studied abroad — unless eligible under CIWG (parent in Gulf)
  • ❌ Anyone without valid JEE Main 2026 rank (SAT is no longer accepted)
  • ❌ Anyone with <75% in Class XII (confirmed: both 2024 and 2025 official brochures require 75% / 7.5 CGPA)
  • ❌ NRI whose child studied in India (Class XI-XII must be from abroad)

Who CAN Realistically Use DASA?

The most common eligible profiles:

  • Indian families settled abroad (US, UK, Canada, Gulf, Singapore, Australia) whose child completed high school abroad and also took JEE Main
  • OCI/PIO card holders — no residential restriction, just need the card + JEE score
  • Foreign nationals of Indian origin who took JEE Main

If the student studied Class XI-XII in India: DASA is only available if they hold OCI/PIO card or foreign passport. NRI status alone is not enough — they must have studied abroad.

DASA Application Process

  1. Check eligibility — confirm FN/OCI/PIO/NRI status and gather documents
  2. Apply on dasanit.org (typically March–May). Separate portal from JAC Delhi.
  3. Upload documents: passport, OCI/PIO card, Class XII marksheet, JEE Main 2026 scorecard, proof of study abroad (for NRI)
  4. Pay application fee (~$200, non-refundable)
  5. Online counselling — 3-4 rounds of seat allotment. Fill college + branch preferences.
  6. Accept seat + pay semester fees in USD via SBI
  7. Report to college with original documents + medical fitness certificate
